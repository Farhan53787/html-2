Bangladeshi Cuisine

It is mainly based on rice and fish, reflecting the riverine geography and rich culture. Spicy curries, bhartas, khichuri and hilsa fish are the main attractions here. There is also Mughal influence in biryani and kebabs, and among sweets, Bogra yogurt and pitha-puli are very popular

Daily Foods

Daily food in Bangladesh is centered around rice, fish, and lentils, typically accompanied by vegetables and fiery curries. Key staples include bhorta (mashed vegetables/fish), bhaji (fried veggies), and varied preparations of rice, such as panta bhat.

Sweets

Bangladeshi cuisine features a rich variety of milk-based and syrup-soaked sweets, with staples including Chomchom, Rasmalai, Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt), and Jilapi.

Street Foods

While, its vibrant street food scene features savory, spicy snacks like Fuchka (stuffed pastry with tamarind water), Jhalmuri (spicy puffed rice), and Samosas/Shingara are widely well-known, foreigners are very interested to come to Bangladesh for taking their tastes.
